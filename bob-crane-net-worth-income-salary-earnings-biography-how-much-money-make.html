Robert Edward “Bob” Crane (July 13, 1928 – June 29, 1978) was an American actor and disc jockey who was born in New York City on July 13, 192Crane started his career as a radio jockey in New York and Connecticut before relocating to Los Angeles, where he presented the city’s most popular morning programme, which was ranked number one by listeners. In the early 1960s, he decided to pursue a career in acting. Crane is best remembered for his role as Colonel Robert E. Hogan in the CBS comedy Hogan’s Heroes, in which he starred as Colonel Robert E. Hogan. The series lasted from 1965 through 1971, and Crane was nominated for two Primetime Emmy Awards for his performance on the show during that period. Crane’s professional life began to deteriorate when Hogan’s Heroes came to an end. He grew dissatisfied with the limited number of parts he was being given and decided to try his hand at dinner theatre.  NBC’s The Bob Crane Show, which premiered in 1975, marked his comeback to television. Due to low viewership, the show was terminated after just 13 weeks on the air. Following that, Crane resumed to acting at dinner theatres and performed in a few guest appearances on television from time to time. Crane was discovered bludgeoned to death in his Scottsdale apartment in June 1978, while on tour for his show Beginner’s Luck. The murder remains unsolved to this day, despite extensive investigation.
